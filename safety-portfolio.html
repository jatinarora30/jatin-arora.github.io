Safety Evaluation Portfolio

This page summarizes my independent work on structured safety evaluation kits for robotic systems. Each kit defines safety intent (invariants), hazard scope, and evidence-based scenarios intended for pre-deployment and internal engineering review.

This work is for portfolio and engineering demonstration purposes. It is not certification or regulatory approval.

Kit
ESTOP-SAFE-v1.0 — Emergency Stop & Safe Stop
Version: v1.0 • Updated: Jan 2026

Evaluates emergency stop response time and stopping distance, override priority across control sources, and prevention of unintended restart after emergency stop release.

  • Robot types: Mobile robots, arms/manipulators, mobile manipulators
  • Core checks: bounded stop, command override, no auto-resume
  • Evidence: logs (timestamps/state), video, repeatability across cycles

Kit
LOAD-HANDLE-v1.0 — Load Handling & Transfer
Version: v1.0 • Updated: Jan 2026

Evaluates load stability during loading, transport, turning/braking, and unloading. Focuses on preventing load drop, unstable handling, and unsafe interaction with people or obstacles during load operations.

  • Robot types: Mobile robots, arms/manipulators, mobile manipulators
  • Core checks: load retention, stability under motion, safe release
  • Evidence: video observations, logs, event timestamps

How I Structure a Kit

  • Safety intent: invariants with clear pass/fail meaning
  • Hazard scope: concrete hazard statements and references where applicable
  • Scenarios: nominal + failure cases with evidence requirements
  • Outputs: findings, gaps, and re-run conditions
